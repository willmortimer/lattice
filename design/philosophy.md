# Crystalline Signal

*An algorithmic philosophy for the Lattice visual identity.*

## The movement

Crystalline Signal treats structure as the artwork and light as the event. Its
subject is the lattice itself — a three-dimensional grid of points and the
edges that join them — projected obliquely so that a cube reads as a diamond:
a hexagonal silhouette standing on its vertex, depth folded into two
dimensions by nothing more than an angle. Nothing in the composition is drawn
freehand. Every line is the consequence of a projection formula; every node
sits where three integer coordinates say it must. The aesthetic conviction is
that a form this constrained can still feel alive, because life enters not
through the geometry but through what travels across it.

## Computation as material

The work is built from one meticulously crafted algorithm, not from shapes.
An axonometric projection maps integer lattice points `(x, y, z)` onto the
plane; a single tilt parameter slides the projection between isometric purity
and dimetric tension. Depth is not faked with gradients but computed: each
edge carries a depth coordinate, and opacity falls away along it, so the far
scaffold of the crystal dissolves into the ground while the near unit cell
stays sharp. The same few constants — grid pitch, subdivision count, fade
curve, node radius — govern everything from a 16-pixel favicon to a
1200-pixel animation, which is the discipline of the movement: one parametric
system, expressed at every scale, refined the way a master typographer
refines a single letterform for years.

## Signal over structure

A lattice with nothing moving through it is a diagram. Crystalline Signal
insists on traffic. Seeded randomness selects a handful of vertices to wake:
they glow, breathe on slow sine phases, and launch pulses that travel the
edges at constant speed, handed from segment to segment like packets through
a switch fabric. The randomness is never decorative noise — it is a seed, and
the same seed always wakes the same nodes and routes the same pulses, so
every variation is reproducible, addressable, and permanent. Between the
frozen crystal and the moving light there is a deliberate hierarchy: the
structure whispers in thin slate lines, and the signal speaks in a single
warm amber. One accent, never two.

## Emergence within order

The compositions that matter are the ones the formula produces on its own.
When the subdivision count rises, moiré densities appear that no one placed;
when the tilt approaches thirty degrees, the near corner of the cube lands
exactly on the silhouette's center, and the brightest node of the mark turns
out to be the point closest to the viewer — an emergent alignment discovered,
not designed, and then kept, the way a jazz player keeps the accident that
was better than the chart. The algorithm was tuned through countless
iterations until such accidents became reliable: every ratio between stroke
weight, node radius, and fade depth is the survivor of painstaking
comparison, the product of deep computational craft rather than taste applied
at the end.

## The seed beneath

Quietly, the system encodes what it decorates. The lattice is a directory of
plain files: every node an addressable resource, every edge a command that
moves change through one core, every glowing vertex a file that something —
a person, a script, an agent — just touched. Those who know the product will
feel the diagram of it inside the ornament; everyone else simply sees a
crystal carrying light. That is the standard of the movement: the reference
must never announce itself, and the craft must be so complete that the
geometry would stand even if the meaning were removed.
